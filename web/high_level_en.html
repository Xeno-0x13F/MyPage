For Masters

This page is mainly for those who are aiming for FC and AP in the MASTER difficulty level,

and will introduce what we think is important in aiming for FC and AP!

Doing score research

When the difficulty level reaches MASTER, the score is not easy to understand on the first sight.

This is where score research is necessary. It is very important to know why you are not doing well.

There are many ways to do this, such as watching videos of AP's hands to check fingering and hitting,

or recording and reviewing your own playing, but I think you can improve a lot just by studying scores.

Think about why you are not doing well and how to improve, and practice with this in mind every time.

One way to help with the difficult parts is to refer to the fingering of someone who is good at it.

Try different fingerings to find the one that suits you best!

(The sense of accomplishment when you get an AP with your own fingerings,

which you made by trial and error, is amazing.)

Change settings to suit your mood or song

I think sometimes the judgments seem to be a little off for some songs.

When aiming for AP, it is out of the question to be misjudged.

Tinker with the ‚ÄúTiming Adjustment‚Äù setting as needed.

Also, as the difficulty level gets higher and the amount of material increases,

it may be a good idea to increase the ‚Äúspeed of the notes‚Äù to reduce visibility.

You can change with or without SE depending on your mood, but there may be some songs

that are easier to play with SE and others that are easier to play without, so try different songs.

(Personally, I find it easier to play songs with a lot of trills and swing rhythms with SE.)
