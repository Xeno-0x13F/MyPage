Manual

On this page, I will introduce some of the attitudes that

I have found important in playing prsk and improving my skills!

Mental health management

The first step is mental health management. The most important thing in playing a game is to enjoy it,

so this is not the time to lose confidence because you can't do it well.

Play the game with confidence and enjoyment on a regular basis.(?)

That said, everyone has times when they falter.

So here are four of my own "ways to stay motivated"!

1, Set small goals.

"FC all EXPERT songs by the end of the year!",

"AP 3 more MASTER songs by the end of this month!" etc...

It is all very well to set goals, but with such large goals,

there is the potential for overwhelming mental damage

if you give up or fail to achieve them along the way.

I'm sure you've all had that experience, right? (Is it just me?)

In such cases, try to set small goals. It doesn't matter how small it is.

By setting and achieving many goals that can be accomplished quickly,

you can keep yourself motivated each day and steadily build your skills.

2, Don't try too hard.

It's already as good as the word. Continuously playing the same score for long periods of time

in pursuit of FC or AP can cause habits to develop.

If you get into the habit, you will make many of the same mistakes in the same places,

‚Üê

overwhelming despair

This is what happens. (Experienced person knows the things)

Sometimes it is important to give up gracefully. If you feel swamped or not doing very well,

stop immediately. (Strategic retreat)

3, Do something else for a change.

If you are not feeling well, turn your attention to anything else.

By daring not to touch a rhythm game for a certain period of time,

you may be able to break the habit and achieve unexpected results the next time you play!

4, Your fav in your eyes.

This is also a kind of mood swings,

but again, favs stabilizes

our spirit just by being in view.

Watch videos of people who play well

Watching videos of people who play well is also a shortcut to improving.

Here are two types of videos to help you improve.

Please look for them on social networking sites and video sites!

Explanatory Videos

It mainly includes explanations of game specifications and their application,

as well as fingering explanations.

Especially for "long notes", there is a big difference between understanding the specifications

and not understanding them, so we recommend that you read the explanation.

Play Videos

Mostly "AP hands", but I also enjoy seeing the ‚Äúfirst reaction and response‚Äù of people who play well.

I also recommend "Play Videos" with explanations,

as it is easy to understand the tips and things to be aware of.

Exposure to many scores

If you play a variety of scores,

you will learn the patterns of the score,

so you will be able to get similar placements

on the first try.
